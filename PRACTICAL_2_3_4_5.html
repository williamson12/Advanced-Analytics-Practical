Tests of Significance – 2 Tests Concerning Single Population Mean¶

One-Sample t-test:¶

Objective:¶

To determine whether the mean of a sample is significantly different from a hypothesized population mean.

Assumptions:¶

  1. The data are independent and identically distributed.

  2. The population from which the sample is drawn is approximately normally distributed.

  3. The standard deviation of the population is unknown.

STEPS:¶

  1. Formulate the null hypothesis (H0) and alternative hypothesis (H1).

  2. Select a significance level (α).

  3. Calculate the test statistic using the formula

  4. Determine the degrees of freedom (df=n−1).

  5. Determine the critical value from the t-distribution table or use statistical software.

  6. Compare the calculated t-statistic with the critical value.

  7. Make a decision:

If |t| > t critical, reject the null hypothesis.

If |t| <= t critical, fail to reject the null hypothesis.

Tests of Significance – 3: Tests Concerning Two Populations Mean¶

Independent Samples t-test:¶

Objective:¶

To determine whether the means of two independent samples are significantly different from each other.

Assumptions:¶

  1. The data in each sample are independent and identically distributed.

  2. Both populations from which the samples are drawn are approximately normally distributed.

  3. The variances of the two populations are equal (homogeneity of variances) or approximately equal (if variances are not equal, a Welch's t-test can be used).

STEPS:¶

  1. Formulate the null hypothesis (H0) and alternative hypothesis (H1).

  2. Select a significance level (α).

  3. Calculate the test statistic using the formula

  4. Determine the degrees of freedom (df = n1 + n2 -2).

  5. Determine the critical value from the t-distribution table or use statistical software.

  6. Compare the calculated t-statistic with the critical value.

  7. Make a decision:

If |t| > t critical, reject the null hypothesis.

If |t| <= t critical, fail to reject the null hypothesis.

Tests of Significance – 4: Tests Concerning Population Variance.¶

Chi-square Test for Variance:¶

Objective:¶

To determine whether the variance of a population is significantly different from a hypothesized value.

Assumptions:¶

  1. The data are independent and identically distributed.

  2. The population from which the sample is drawn follows a normal distribution.

  3. The sample is randomly selected.

Steps:¶

  1. Formulate the null hypothesis (H0) and alternative hypothesis (H1).

  2. Select a significance level (α).

  3. Calculate the sample variance ( S^2)

  4. Calculate the chi-square statistic using the formula

  5. Determine the degrees of freedom (df=n−1)

  6. Determine the critical value from the chi-square distribution table or use statistical software.

  7. Compare the calculated chi-square statistic with the critical value.

  8. Make a decision:

If the calculated chi-square statistic is greater than the critical value, reject the null hypothesis.

If the calculated chi-square statistic is less than or equal to the critical value, fail to reject the null hypothesis.
